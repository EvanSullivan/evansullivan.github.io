Evan Sullivan

User Experience Designer

I enjoy helping to make life better for others by building useful, pleasant, and easy-to-use software.

My work to date helps people conveniently interact with their car, be informed about the risk of sun exposure, get tech help, and even enjoy some absurd fun.

Learn more about my past work with the selected projects below.

MY WORK:

HondaLink & AcuraLink

2018-present

The HondaLink and AcuraLink mobile apps enable people to remotely interact with and see the status of their vehicle.

This service also connects with Amazon to enable customers to have packages delivered directly inside their vehicle.

I am the Senior UX Designer for both HondaLink and AcuraLink.

Sun Risk

2015-present

Sun Risk is an app for iOS which uses dynamic color and typography to inform users of their risk of skin damage from the sun.

I designed and implemented the iOS app using Swift and Xcode. I also built the marketing website.

Honda R&D

2016-2018

The focus of my work at Honda R&D was prototyping in-vehicle interfaces that make sense in the context of autonomous driving.
I wish I could show the projects I worked on here, but given the nature of R&D, it's all got to be kept under wraps!

My work here primarily involved integrating findings from academic research into functional interface prototypes.

2015

The UCLA Service-Now website allows users to submit IT help requests with a quick and intuitive menu system.

Quick, Press A!

2014

Quick, Press A! is a satirical iOS game in which players must mash an oversized ‘A’ button fast enough to stay alive.

I designed and produced all art assets for the game. I also implemented the UI using C# in Unity.
